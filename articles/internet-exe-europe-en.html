The dossier · why

We talk about sovereignty.
Let's start by remembering.

We keep saying the Americans won the Internet. That's true today. It wasn't always.

In the early 2000s, the Internet looked like an archipelago: distinct islands, separate languages, different owners — and a good share of those islands were European. In Portugal, you searched on SAPO, born in 1995 at the University of Aveiro: six students who had indexed the Portuguese-language web before the term "search engine" meant much of anything. By 1999 it was the most visited site in the country. Every country had its SAPO.

At the time, nobody saw it as an era. It was just the Internet.

This dossier tells the story of that archipelago in four parts: what we did on it, the map of the giants we lost, why the bubble of 2000 may have broken European ambition, and what all of it says about our second chance — AI. We open with the most accessible part: a single day, then and now.

P.S. — your old Nokia still works.
